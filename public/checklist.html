🚨 Contractor Red Flags Checklist

23 Warning Signs That Could Save You Thousands

From QuoteEvaluator.com - Your trusted quote analysis partner

🔍 BEFORE HIRING: Initial Red Flags

❌ 1. Door-to-Door Solicitation
Red Flag: Contractor shows up unannounced offering "leftover materials"
Why It's Bad: Legitimate contractors are busy with scheduled work
Average overpay: 200-400%
❌ 2. No Physical Business Address
Red Flag: Only provides P.O. Box or cell phone number
Why It's Bad: Makes it impossible to pursue legal action
Real Cost: $15,000+ lost to fly-by-night operators
❌ 3. Requests Full Payment Upfront
Red Flag: Demands 100% payment before starting work
Why It's Bad: No incentive to complete or do quality work
Standard: Never pay more than 10% upfront, 30% max
❌ 4. No Written Contract
Red Flag: Wants to proceed with handshake deals only
Why It's Bad: No legal protection for you
Real Cost: $25,000+ in disputes with no documentation
❌ 5. Unusually Low Bids
Red Flag: Quote is 50%+ lower than other estimates
Why It's Bad: Usually indicates cut corners or bait-and-switch
Hidden Costs: Change orders can double the final price

📋 LICENSING & INSURANCE RED FLAGS

❌ 6. Can't Provide License Number
Red Flag: Avoids showing contractor's license
Why It's Bad: Unlicensed work voids permits and insurance
Verification: Always check with state licensing board
❌ 7. No Liability Insurance
Red Flag: Can't provide certificate of insurance
Why It's Bad: You're liable for accidents on your property
Minimum: $1M general liability + workers' comp
❌ 8. Expired or Invalid Insurance
Red Flag: Insurance documents are outdated
Why It's Bad: Claims will be denied
Action: Call insurance company to verify coverage

💰 PRICING & PAYMENT RED FLAGS

❌ 9. Vague "Labor and Materials" Pricing
Red Flag: No itemized breakdown of costs
Why It's Bad: Impossible to verify fair pricing
Example: "$4,500 for labor" (what kind? how many hours?)
❌ 10. Cash-Only Payment
Red Flag: Refuses checks or credit cards
Why It's Bad: Tax evasion + no payment protection
Benefit Loss: No credit card dispute rights
❌ 11. Pressure for Immediate Decision
Red Flag: "This price is only good today"
Why It's Bad: High-pressure sales tactic
Reality: Good contractors are booked weeks ahead

💡 BONUS: Questions to Ask Every Contractor

  1. "Can you provide your license number for verification?"
  2. "May I see your current insurance certificate?"
  3. "Can you provide 3 references from the last 6 months?"
  4. "What warranty do you provide on materials and labor?"
  5. "Will you be pulling the permits, and in whose name?"
  6. "What's your payment schedule?"
  7. "Who will be doing the actual work - your employees or subcontractors?"
  8. "What happens if we discover additional issues during the project?"

✅ GREEN FLAGS: What Good Contractors Do

🟢 Proper Documentation

  • Licensed and insured (with verification)
  • Written contracts with detailed specifications
  • Fixed timeline with milestones
  • Clear change order process

🟢 Professional Business Practices

  • Physical business address and showroom
  • Professional website and marketing materials
  • Detailed itemized quotes
  • References from recent customers

🎯 Take Action: Your Next Steps

Before You Hire:

  • Verify license with state board
  • Call insurance company to confirm coverage
  • Check Better Business Bureau rating
  • Get 3+ written quotes for comparison
  • Visit their business location

Red Flag Score:

  • 0-2 Red Flags: Proceed with caution
  • 3-5 Red Flags: High risk - get second opinion
  • 6+ Red Flags: Walk away immediately
